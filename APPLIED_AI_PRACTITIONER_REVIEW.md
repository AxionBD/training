## 📌 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The **Applied AI Practitioner Program** (4-week weekend/evening program) has strong potential but requires **curriculum expansion and clearer positioning**. Current design is too compressed for beginners but could work well for those with basic Python/IT knowledge.

**Key Finding:** Program needs 5–6 weeks (not 4) to properly cover all topics OR requires clear Python prerequisites.

**Recommendation:** Expand to 5 weeks + add job placement support to maximize ROI for students.

## ⚠️ CRITICAL ISSUES & GAPS

### **ISSUE #1: Week 1 is Severely Compressed** ⚠️⚠️⚠️

**Current Week 1 Topics:**
- AI concepts & ecosystem
- Data fundamentals (NumPy, Pandas, data types, cleaning)
- Python basics (syntax, OOP, libraries)
- All in 1 week (4–6 hours)

**Problem:**
- Too much ground to cover in 1 week
- Absolute beginners will struggle
- Not enough hands-on practice time
- May lose students early

**Real-World Example:**
- Learning Python syntax alone: 8–12 hours
- Data fundamentals (NumPy + Pandas): 8–12 hours
- AI concepts: 4–6 hours
- **Total needed: 20–30 hours** (not 4–6 hours)

**Recommendation:**
- **Option A:** Expand Week 1 to 2 weeks (split Python & Data Fundamentals)
- **Option B:** Assume students have Python basics (add prerequisite)
- **Option C:** Offer "Pre-program Python crash course" (1 week, optional)

---

### **ISSUE #2: Week 3 (Generative AI) is Compressed** ⚠️⚠️

**Current Week 3 Topics:**
- LLM concepts (4–6 hours)
- Prompt Engineering (4–6 hours)
- Chatbot systems (4–6 hours)
- RAG architecture (4–6 hours)
- **Total:** 2 weeks of content in 1 week

**Problem:**
- RAG is complex and needs deeper exploration
- Prompt engineering deserves more hands-on time
- Students won't have time to build meaningful LLM applications
- No time to explore fine-tuning, custom GPTs, or advanced prompting

**Recommendation:**
- Expand Week 3 to **1.5–2 weeks**
- Split into: "GenAI Basics" (LLM concepts, prompt engineering) + "Advanced GenAI" (RAG, chatbots, fine-tuning)

## 📊 PROPOSED 5-WEEK CURRICULUM (RECOMMENDED)

### **Week 1: Python Fundamentals (8 hours)**
**Topics:**
- Python basics (syntax, data types, control flow)
- Functions, modules, packages
- Object-oriented programming (classes, methods)
- Debugging & best practices

**Lab:**
- Build 5 mini-projects (calculator, game, etc.)
- Emphasis on clean code & documentation

---

### **Week 2: Data Science Fundamentals (8 hours)**
**Topics:**
- NumPy: arrays, operations, linear algebra
- Pandas: DataFrames, cleaning, transformation
- Matplotlib & Seaborn: visualization
- Statistical concepts for ML

**Lab:**
- Real data exploration project
- Data cleaning & EDA (Exploratory Data Analysis)
- Create visualizations from datasets

---

### **Week 3: Machine Learning (12 hours)**
**Topics:**
- Supervised learning concepts
- Regression models (linear, polynomial, ridge, lasso)
- Classification models (logistic regression, decision trees, random forest, SVM)
- Model evaluation (accuracy, precision, recall, F1, ROC-AUC)
- Hyperparameter tuning
- Cross-validation

**Lab:**
- Build 2–3 complete ML projects
- Kaggle competition (optional)
- Model comparison & selection

---

### **Week 4: Generative AI & LLMs (12 hours)**
**Topics (Part A):**
- How LLMs work (transformer architecture, tokens, attention)
- Popular LLMs (GPT-4, Claude, Llama)
- Prompt engineering techniques
- Few-shot learning & chain-of-thought

**Topics (Part B):**
- Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
- Vector databases (Pinecone, Chroma, Weaviate)
- Building chatbots with LLMs
- Fine-tuning & custom models

**Lab:**
- Advanced prompt engineering exercises
- Build RAG system with custom data
- Create multi-turn chatbot
- Deploy chatbot to Streamlit/Discord

---

### **Week 5: AI Applications, Ethics & Capstone (12 hours)**

**Part A: Applications & Deployment (6 hours)**
- AI APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Cohere)
- Building AI applications (web apps, chatbots, dashboards)
- Cloud deployment (Heroku, AWS, Google Cloud)
- Monitoring & maintenance

**Part B: Ethics & Responsible AI (3 hours)**
- AI bias & fairness
- Privacy & security in AI
- Responsible AI governance
- Ethical considerations

**Part C: Capstone Presentations (3 hours)**
- Student project presentations
- Peer feedback
- Final refinements

## 📅 DELIVERY MODEL OPTIONS

### **Option 1: Weekend Bootcamp (Recommended)**
- **Schedule:** Saturday 9 AM – 5 PM, Sunday 10 AM – 2 PM
- **Hours/week:** 9 hours
- **Total:** 5 weekends, ~45 hours
- **Pros:** Full immersion, social bonding, dedicated learning
- **Cons:** Less time for homework, burnout risk

---

### **Option 2: Evening Program**
- **Schedule:** Tuesday & Thursday 7 PM – 10 PM
- **Hours/week:** 6 hours
- **Total:** 10 weeks (extended timeline)
- **Pros:** Minimal impact on day job, sustainable pace
- **Cons:** Long program, higher dropout risk, fatigue

---

### **Option 3: Hybrid (Weekend + Async)**
- **Schedule:** Saturday 10 AM – 1 PM (live) + pre-recorded content
- **Async:** 3–4 hours/week self-paced learning
- **Pros:** Flexibility, reinforced learning, asynchronous recordings
- **Cons:** Requires discipline from students

---

### **Option 4: Full-Time Intensive (2 weeks)**
- **Schedule:** Daily 10 AM – 5 PM
- **Hours:** 35–40 hours/week
- **Total:** 2 weeks, ~75 hours
- **Pros:** Fast completion, deep immersion
- **Cons:** Very intense, potential burnout, less homework time

**RECOMMENDATION:** **Option 1 (Weekend Bootcamp)** - Best balance of intensity and flexibility
